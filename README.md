# Case Study: E-Commerce Web Design for Gold and Girl Honey
**Location:** Queensland, Australia 

---

## Introduction
This case study outlines the end-to-end UX/UI design process for "Gold and Girl," a local Queensland business specialising in raw, chemical-free honey. The goal of this project is to build a distinctive desktop web presence that balances a premium, hand-crafted brand identity with critical environmental advocacy. By addressing real-world ecosystem threats alongside commercial objectives, the resulting platform engages conscious consumers and establishes deep community roots.

---

## 1. The Empathise Phase

### Business Owner Insights
I approached the business owner to gain insight into the design elements and psychological connections tied to honey. This collaborative discovery ensures the final product reflects the brand's authentic message while matching customer expectations.

### Competitive Market Analysis
I analysed existing honey and artisanal websites to identify common marketing trends, content strategies, and structural patterns.

#### Typography
*   **Artisanal Aesthetic:** Competitor sites consistently leverage fonts that convey a homemade, hand-crafted feel.

#### Color Palette
*   **Warm Tones:** Deep ambers, golden yellows, and rich caramels directly mimic the natural appearance of honey.
*   **Earthy Neutrals:** Soft creams, muted greens (such as eucalyptus), and warm browns establish a natural, organic environment.
*   **High Contrast:** Crisp white backgrounds keep text highly legible while forcing the golden hues to pop.

#### Layout Trends
*   **Grid Systems:** Used systematically in shop sections to neatly organize jar sizes, flavors, and product lines.
*   **Asymmetrical & Organic Structures:** Breaking away from blocky, rigid modules by using overlapping images to mimic the fluid nature of an apiary.
*   **Above-the-Fold Banners:** High-resolution headers displaying the farm or product to instantly communicate the business's core purpose.

#### Content & Identity Markers
*   **Origin Stories:** Most small businesses prominently feature a "Get to Know the Beekeeper" narrative.
*   **Earthy Visual Signifiers:** Heavy use of amber and soft greens to signify organic, unadulterated products.
*   **Transparent Labelling:** Clear callouts highlighting what makes the honey unique, such as "Unfiltered", "100% Raw", regional origins, and specific floral/plant sources.

#### Geometric Motifs & Shapes
*   **Hexagons:** The most common structural motif, directly referencing natural honeycomb patterns.
*   **Honey Drips:** Stylized flowing vectors used as decorative section dividers, borders, and header accents.
*   **Soft Curves:** Rounded corners on buttons and image boxes to maintain a friendly, approachable, non-corporate presence.

#### Visual Asset Trends
*   **Product Photography:** High-quality, warm-lit imagery of hexagonal glass jars styled against timber or floral backdrops.
*   **Action Shots:** Macro photography showcasing honey dippers lifting golden syrup or honeycombs oozing fresh harvest.
*   **Behind-the-Scenes Media:** Authentic documentation of active apiaries, beehives, floral fields, and suit-clad beekeepers extracting raw honey.

#### Video Integration
*   **Harvesting B-Roll:** Short, looping background video clips showcasing raw honey extraction.
*   **Product Utility:** Cinematic macros showing honey drizzled over morning toast, teas, or culinary recipes.
*   **Founder Welcomes:** Short greeting videos from the business owners to humanize the brand and thank users for supporting local trade.

## 2. The Design Phase

### Problem Statement
> **Gold and Girl** needs a web presence that showcases raw, chemical-free honey byproducts and highlights community environmental action because the imminent Varroa mite invasion threatens QLD bee populations, making local ecosystem awareness and sustainable, plastic-free production vital for conscious consumers.

### Project Scope

#### What the Website WILL Include
*   **The Main Home Section:** A clean layout presenting raw honey items alongside explicit product rules: *"No Chemicals, No Heating, No Plastic"*.
*   **The Bee Crisis Banner:** A dedicated informational segment explaining the Varroa mite threat and listing regional flora that supports local bee conservation.
*   **The Top Navigation Bar:** A styled, highly functional header component designed to streamline cross-sectional navigation.
*   **A Custom Scrollbar:** A distinct, branded sidebar track styled to replace the default browser UI wrapper.

#### What the Website WON'T Include
*   **Mobile Layouts:** No responsive breakpoints or smartphone-specific code blocks will be written, as this iteration serves strictly as a desktop evaluation test.
*   **A Real Shopping Cart:** Active checkout workflows, user profile registration modules, and transactional payment gateways are excluded.
*   **A Live Forum:** No interactive public comment channels or digital community message boards that require active moderation.

### Measurable Design Criteria & Verification Methods

#### Criterion 1: Layout Organization and Visual Theme
*   **The Rule:** The homepage must utilize a structured layout format (featuring a Flexbox navigation bar and distinct semantic divisions) and embed exactly three industry-specific visual assets (a honeycomb grid, a bee graphic, and a natural flora motif).
*   **Verification Method:** Inspect the compiled codebase architecture and manually audit the rendered desktop screen interface to verify the layout parameters and count the matching thematic elements (Pass/Fail).

#### Criterion 2: Typography and Color Contrast
*   **The Rule:** Body copy must utilize a legible font configuration that reinforces an artisanal, homemade identity while maintaining sufficient color contrast ratios against background elements to prevent user eye strain.
*   **Verification Method:** Audit the active CSS typography rulesets and visually evaluate the rendered canvas for overall readability (Pass/Fail).

#### Criterion 3: Call-to-Action (CTA) Prominence
*   **The Rule:** The community awareness section must feature a dedicated action button utilizing a distinct, high-contrast accent color separated from the core theme values to immediately funnel user focus toward the Varroa mite resource materials.
*   **Verification Method:** Run a visual target check on the live desktop interface to ensure the awareness button is instantly distinguished by its accent coloring (Pass/Fail).

## 3. The Ideate Phase

### Asset Generation & Mood Boarding
*   **Palette Discovery:** Used *Coolors.co* to engineer a custom color scheme matching raw organic honey and natural Queensland ecosystems.
*   **Typography Selection:** Sourced fonts from *Google Fonts* that balance professional commercial legibility with hand-crafted, artisanal identity traits.
*   **Inspiration Gathering:** Collected raw texture samples, hexagonal jar profiles, macro bee photography, and clean organic labeling designs to reinforce the 100% natural product claim.

### Wireframing & Design Selection
I developed three distinct wireframe variations to explore layout structures and ultimately selected **Wireframe 1** as the final design blueprint.

#### Rationale for Selecting Wireframe 1
*   **Mental Model Alignment:** Follows industry conventions by placing the brand identification and structural links side-by-side at the pixel absolute top, followed immediately by an impact-driven Hero section.
*   **Intuitive Information Flow:** Users naturally absorb the brand identity, contextualize the main product offering via the Hero display, read supporting mission statements upon scrolling, and conclude with the critical environmental call-to-action right before the footer.
*   **Engagement Optimization:** This structured layout prioritizes storytelling hierarchy, resulting in a smooth, highly predictable desktop browsing experience.

---

## What's Next?
Following the successful compilation of the desktop wireframes and core design foundations, the next phase focuses on transforming this prototype into a full, production-ready web platform.

*   **Real Shopping Section Page:** Upgrading the application architecture to support a live e-commerce engine. This will include interactive product grid layouts, real-time stock filters for jar sizes, an active shopping cart overlay, and a secure payment gateway integration for real transactions.
*   **Contact Us Section:** Implementing a dedicated contact directory. This module will feature an automated email enquiry form, map coordinates highlighting the local apiary, and a community resource submission tool allowing locals to report area environmental efforts or seek bee conservation guidance.
